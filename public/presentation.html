Class Imbalance — Why Accuracy Alone Misleads

Training Set Distribution

No Direct Debit 79%
Direct Debit 21%

Imbalance ratio: 3.76 : 1 (285,696 vs. 76,028 in train set)

Naive baseline trap

A model that always predicts "No Direct Debit" would reach ~79% accuracy without learning anything useful.

Our Strategy

  • class_weight='balanced' on all sklearn classifiers to penalise majority class
  • scale_pos_weight = 3.76 for XGBoost to match imbalance ratio
  • Primary metrics: ROC-AUC, F1-Score, Recall
  • Stratified train/test split to preserve class proportions

Modelling

Five Classifiers — Rationale

Decision Tree Depth 5

Highly interpretable — splits translate directly to business rules. Used as explainability model for broker training. Risk: prone to overfitting without depth constraints.

Random Forest 200 Trees

Ensemble averaging reduces overfitting. Provides feature importances for business interpretation. Slower on very large datasets.

Logistic Regression Baseline

Strong interpretable baseline — coefficients show direction and magnitude. Ideal for communicating to non-technical stakeholders. Assumes linear decision boundary.

SVM (RBF Kernel) 50K Sample

Captures non-linear decision boundaries. Trained on stratified 50K sample due to O(n²) computational cost at full 360K+ scale. Test set remains the full 90K.

XGBoost ★ Selected Model

Gradient boosting iteratively corrects residual errors. Best in class on tabular data with sparse one-hot encoded features. 300 estimators, learning_rate=0.05, max_depth=4. scale_pos_weight=3.76 handles class imbalance natively.

Data Leakage Discovery — Strategy B Analysis

Finding

Payment_frequency = Monthly is near-perfectly correlated with direct debit because of an Allianz business rule: monthly contracts must use direct debit. This is a rule, not a learnable pattern — it is near-total data leakage.

Interpretation

  • Strategy A is valid for scoring existing contracts where payment frequency is known.
  • Strategy B (AUC 75.56%) reveals the genuine behavioural and demographic signals — customer segment, region, product type, broker quality — that drive voluntary adoption.
  • For campaign targeting of non-monthly contracts, Strategy B is the operationally meaningful model.

A/B Pilot Structure

Top-ranked contracts by model score — targeted outreach

5,000

Randomly selected non-DD contracts — no outreach

Duration: 90 days

Success metric: Δ conversion rate (treatment − control)

Output: Calibrate model threshold, retraining trigger

Why A Controlled Pilot First

  • Validates predicted lift against actual campaign response before full portfolio rollout
  • Avoids burning marketing budget on low-probability contracts
  • Generates labelled response data for uplift modelling in Phase 2
  • Allows threshold calibration — should the cut-off be 50%, 30%, or 70%?
  • Builds internal confidence in the model before CFO-level decisions
Expected economic signal:

If the Top 5K group converts at even 15% vs. 5% in control, that is 500 new direct debit contracts from a single campaign cycle.

Recommendations

Segment-Level Campaign Strategy

Retail Individuals

Non-monthly payers are the most natural conversion target. Already comfortable with recurring digital payments. Direct outreach via email / app notification is most effective.

SME & Mid-Corp

Finance-driven — prefer invoice control. Approach via account managers and brokers. Messaging should focus on reduced admin workload and payment certainty, not convenience.

Quarterly Payers

Already accept periodic auto-payment — strongest next-step conversion pool. Target at renewal with a single-click DD switch offer.

Broker Engagement Strategy

  • Share per-broker DD conversion dashboards benchmarked against market average
  • Train brokers to raise DD at policy renewal conversations
  • Introduce quality service incentives tied to DD adoption rate per broker
  • Auto & Property lines: highest structural potential — prioritise these lines in broker briefings

Limitations & Next Steps

Known Limitations

Payment Frequency Leakage

Monthly → DD correlation reflects a business rule, not a learnable behavioural signal. Strategy A results should be interpreted with this caveat. Strategy B (AUC 75.56%) is the true measure of voluntary adoption predictability.

Static Dataset Snapshot

The model reflects one point in time. Client behaviour, product mix, and broker portfolios evolve. Quarterly retraining is required to maintain predictive power.

No Campaign Response History

We predict propensity to use DD — not incremental lift from outreach. A true uplift model requires A/B test outcomes, which the pilot will generate.

Broker_cor Data Quality

Values up to 6 × 10¹⁶ indicate extraction/encoding errors. P95 cap is a pragmatic workaround — the data engineering team should investigate the root cause.
